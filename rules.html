RULES

  • This hackathon is a team event, where teams of 3 to 5 members from any department and any year of study, including both male and female participants, can participate

  • Participants must take one of the project tracks, and all projects must include programming

  • Only one member of the team needs to register, but details of all team members and project proposals must be submitted during registration

  • Project proposals must follow the guidelines provided in the template, which can be downloaded on the home page

  • Teams will only have one opportunity to submit their idea once the hackathon begins, and plagiarism will result in disqualification

  • Late submissions will not be accepted

  • All hack projects must be developed during the hackathon duration and teams may use open source libraries and other freely available systems and services such as Google Maps and Facebook Connect

  • The intellectual property of the code belongs solely to the team
